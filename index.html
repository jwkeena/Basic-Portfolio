About Me

 

I am a front-end web developer, philosopher, and Classicist based in Manchester, New Hampshire. My languages of choice are JavaScript, HTML, CSS, Latin, and Ancient Greek. I have years of experience teaching undergraduates, studying the humanities, writing and editing papers, and, more recently, coding web applications. I have an eye for detail, a mind for complex ideas and languages, a passion for online interactivity, and an interest in making precise, syntactical, logical sense.

I am currently enrolled at the University of New Hampshire's Full Stack Web Development program, learning a range of technologies including Git, jQuery, Node.js, Express.js, React.js, MongoDB, and MySQL. My previous academic training includes a Master of Studies in Ancient Philosophy from the University of Oxford, a Master of Arts in English, and a Bachelor of Arts in Philosophy and Classics. If you want a developer who understands the importance not only of SEO, UX/UI design, and programming syntax, but also the grammar of human languages, the logic of critical thinking, and the rhetoric of online content, I'm your best choice.

When I'm not coding and learning new technologies at UNH, you can find me teaching Ancient Greek Philosophy at Nashua Community College, or at home playing yet another round of the mesmerizing and beautiful Tetris Effect...when my family lets me.
